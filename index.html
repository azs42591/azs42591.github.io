I have lived in Georgia my entire life and have actually only been west of the Mississippi River three times. Almost all of my family lives in Georgia, but the rest are not far, living in North Carolina and Tennessee. I am the oldest of three brothers, and I also have a younger step-sister. I grew up in a Christian home, where I was encouraged and, in a way, expected, to succeed. And I like to think that I have thus far. I graduated from my high school among the top 25 percent of my class and have maintained my academic success through my first year and a half of college, currently holding a 4.0 GPA. In addition to my academic success, I have also greatly excelled in my jobs, earning raises and promotions in almost all of them, and earning them relatively quickly. This has allowed me to garner not only the respect of my coworkers, but the trust of my supervisors, which allows me more flexibility in my work.

Both of my parents graduated from the University of Georgia in 1993, and I was thus born into loving the school and its sports teams, particularly football. I have many fond memories of countless fall Saturdays spent both here in Athens with my family, or at home enjoying the games from afar. As I grew older, I began looking for colleges to attend and I could think of no other place than UGA. I had dreamed of attending school here for four years, but those dreams were temporarily put on hold as I spent my freshman year at the University of North Georgia in Dahlonega. I applied to transfer in January of 2018 and was accepted only a month later. I began classes and August and plan on being enrolled at UGA until I graduate. Luckily, I had over a full semester's worth of AP credits I earned in high school, which allowed me to apply (and be accepted to) the Terry College of Business this fall but will also allow me to graduate at least a semester early in December of 2020.

With a degree in finance and minor in sports management, I plan on finding a job in a professional sports team's front office. Being from Atlanta, I'd love to be able to work for the Falcons or Braves, but as someone once told me, In this industry, you're constantly on the move. Realistically, I will take what I can get, but I hope to build enough experience and connections that I can end up working wherever I want.
